His influences

Marshall Bruce Mathers III or"Eminem" used his troubled life as inspiration for most of his songs; from his bad childhood, failed marriage, and battle with violence and drugs. Here are some examples.

  • "Cleanin' Out My Closet" tells his childhood and his hate for his mother
  • "Not Afraid" describes his battle with drugs and violence
  • "Love the Way You Lie" featured Rihanna and both singers shared diffcult relationships
  • "Headlights" was his way of saying sorry to his mom after "Cleanin' Out My Closet"
  • "Mockingbird" is an account of his early relationship with his wife and daughters, his divorce, and the beginning of his fame

His backstory

Eminem's life was hard and painful one. His parents moved from one place to another, rarely staying in one place. His father left when he was an infant. He spent most of his childhood in predominately black Detroit neighborhoods. Black youths frequently beated him up and in one instance he suffered brain trauma. Since school,he began to become fascinated in rap and participated in competitions in school. Substance abuse resulted in an attempted suicide further strained his marriage. Over the years, he began partnering with many famous stars like Dr. Dre and Ice Cube. he got into conflict with many famous celebtities since many of songs have jabs in the lyrics. He even got almost arrested by the US Secret Service because his song "We As Americans" sounded much like a threat to the president although it wasn't.

His impact

He has been widely considered as one of the greatest hip-hop artists of all time. He has influenced many famous rappers such as 50 Cent, D12, and Stat Quo.
